Getting Started

Your FAM Study Plan

A four-phase roadmap that gets you from a blank page to a passing mock — built so the order matters more than the exact week count.

📖 300+ hours · two subjects in one ⏱️ 16-week standard track 🔑 Learn → Drill → Mix → Mock

This is your roadmap. It assumes you're starting from scratch on the material but already passed P and FM. Adjust the pace to your real timeline — the order matters more than the exact weeks.

How long do I need?

A common, realistic guideline is 300+ hours total for FAM (it's two subjects in one). Pick the track that matches your situation:

Set your exam date first

Register for a sitting (March / July / November) and count backwards. A real deadline is the single biggest driver of getting this done.

The phase structure (this is the key idea)

Every successful FAM plan moves through four phases. Don't skip ahead — each builds on the last.

Phase 1: LEARN        →  Phase 2: DRILL        →  Phase 3: MIX         →  Phase 4: MOCK
(read + worked egs)      (topic-by-topic           (mixed timed sets,      (full 34Q timed
                          problem sets)             both halves)            exams, review)
~50% of time             ~25%                      ~15%                    ~10%
Problems are where the learning happens

The mistake almost everyone makes: spending 90% of their time in Phase 1 (reading) and running out of time for Phases 2–4 (problems). Problems are where the learning actually happens. Start doing problems within the first week.

16-Week Standard Plan

The weekly loop

Each week: read the listed topic note(s) in this folder → work all the worked examples by hand → do the self-check → then do practice problems on that topic → update 05-Progress-Tracker.md.

Phase 1 — Learn (Weeks 1–9)

We lead with the Long-Term (FAM-L) half, because life contingencies is sequential — each topic depends on the previous one — so it rewards an unbroken run. Then we do Short-Term, which is more modular.

Phase 3 — Mix (Weeks 13–14)

  • Daily mixed, timed problem sets pulling from both halves.
  • Hammer your weak topics (your Progress Tracker tells you which).
  • Re-do every problem you've ever missed. Keep a running mistake log.
  • Memorize/internalize the formula sheets in 03-Formula-Sheets/.

Phase 4 — Mock (Weeks 15–16)

  • Full-length, 34-question, 3.5-hour timed mock exams under real conditions (TI-30XS only, FAM tables only, no notes).
  • Review every mock thoroughly — the review is worth more than the exam itself.
  • Final week: light review, formula sheets, fix lingering weak spots, rest before exam day. Do not cram new material in the last 3 days.

Compressed 10–12 week version

Same phase order, just faster: spend ~3–4 days per topic note, start mixed sets by week 7, and reserve the last 2 weeks for mocks. Cut nothing — just move quicker and accept slightly less repetition.

A good daily session (≈3 hours)

  1. 5 min — review your mistake log from last session.
  2. 45 min — read/learn new material (work the examples by hand).
  3. 90 min — practice problems (timed once you're past Phase 1).
  4. 20 min — review what you got wrong; add to mistake log.
  5. 5 min — update 05-Progress-Tracker.md.

Weekly rhythm

  • 5–6 study days, 1 rest day.
  • 1 day per week is "catch-up + weak-topic" — no new material, just shore up gaps.
  • End each week by glancing at the Progress Tracker: is any topic still red?

Readiness check — am I ready to sit?

Sit the exam when all of these are true:

Green-light checklist
  • You've worked through every topic note and its self-checks.
  • You score consistently 70%+ on mixed, timed problem sets.
  • You've completed at least 3 full mock exams at passing level under real conditions.
  • The formula sheets hold no surprises — you can reproduce the key formulas from memory.
  • You're finishing mocks within the time limit with a few minutes to spare.

If you use Coaching Actuaries ADAPT, an Earned Level of ~6–7+ is a widely-used "ready" signal.
