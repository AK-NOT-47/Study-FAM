## 🎯 What is Exam FAM?

FAM is the SOA exam that comes **after Exams P and FM** on the path to becoming an Associate of the Society of Actuaries (ASA). It tests two big areas:

| Half | Nickname | What it covers |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **Short-Term** | FAM-S | Loss modeling (severity/frequency/aggregate), coverage modifications, estimation, credibility, ratemaking, reserving, option pricing |
| **Long-Term** | FAM-L | Life contingencies: survival models, life insurance & annuities, premiums, reserves, multiple-state models |

### Exam format (as of 2026)
- **34 multiple-choice questions**, **3.5 hours**, computer-based (CBT)
- Offered **3 times a year**: ~March, July, November
- **Scored 0–10; a grade of 6 or higher passes.** No penalty for wrong answers — *never leave a blank.*
- Calculator: **TI-30XS MultiView** (the one to practice with)
- You get a provided set of **FAM Tables** (distributions + the Standard Ultimate Life Table)

## 🚀 The 4-step way to use this folder

1. **Orient** — finish this README, then read [00-Getting-Started/Exam-Overview.md](00-Getting-Started/Exam-Overview.md) and [00-Getting-Started/Study-Plan.md](00-Getting-Started/Study-Plan.md).
2. **Learn one topic at a time** — open a topic note, read it, work the examples *with pencil and calculator*, then do the self-check.
3. **Drill** — once a topic clicks, do practice problems on it ([04-Practice/](04-Practice/)). Log every miss.
4. **Track** — update [05-Progress-Tracker.md](05-Progress-Tracker.md) after every session. Mixed timed sets → full mocks in the final weeks.

> **Golden rule:** FAM is *not* a reading exam. You pass it by **doing problems**. Aim for ~70% of your time solving problems, ~30% reading. Reading without practicing is the #1 reason people fail.
