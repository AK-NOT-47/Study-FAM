# Resources — Textbooks, Tables & Question Banks

You do **not** need to buy everything. The single most important resources are **(1) a study manual** and **(2) a large bank of practice problems**. Below is the full landscape so you can choose.

## 🆓 Free & official (get these first — they're mandatory)

| Resource | Why | Where |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **FAM syllabus (your sitting)** | Defines exactly what's tested | soa.org → Exam FAM → Studying |
| **FAM exam tables** | The distribution tables + **SULT** you'll have on exam day — *practice with these, not your own* | soa.org FAM study page |
| **SOA FAM sample questions & solutions** | Real exam-style MCQs from the SOA itself | soa.org FAM study page |
| **SOA past STAM / MLC / LTAM questions** | FAM is built from the old STAM (short-term) and MLC/LTAM (long-term) exams — huge free problem bank that overlaps heavily | soa.org exam archives |

> ⚠️ **Always download the tables for your exam date and use them while practicing.** Knowing where each value lives saves real minutes on exam day.

## 📕 Required / source textbooks (the syllabus readings)

The SOA syllabus draws from these. You can study from the textbooks directly, but most candidates use a study manual (below) that distills them.

- **Loss Models: From Data to Decisions** — Klugman, Panjer, Willmot *(short-term: severity/frequency/aggregate, estimation, credibility)*
- **Actuarial Mathematics for Life Contingent Risks (AMLCR)** — Dickson, Hardy, Waters *(long-term: all of life contingencies)*
- **Fundamentals of General Insurance Actuarial Analysis** — Friedland *(ratemaking & reserving)*
- An SOA **option-pricing study note** *(the small derivatives/option-pricing piece)*

> Confirm exact editions and chapter readings on the syllabus for **your** sitting.

## 📗 Study manuals (highly recommended — pick one ecosystem)

A manual condenses the textbooks into exam-focused lessons + problems. Any of these is excellent; don't overthink the choice.

- **Coaching Actuaries** — manual + **ADAPT** (adaptive problem engine with "Earned Level" tracking). The ADAPT problem bank is the most popular prep tool for these exams.
- **ACTEX** — study manuals (incl. Ambrose Lo's well-regarded FAM-S manual) and a question bank.
- **ASM** — long-established manuals.

> You generally only need **one** manual. The differentiator that matters most is **the size and quality of the practice-problem bank** — that's what you'll live in during Phases 2–4.

## 🧮 Calculator

- **TI-30XS / TI-30X MultiView** — recommended; learn its table/stats features (it speeds up frequency/severity and MLE calcs).
- The **BA II Plus** (and Professional) is also approved and useful for time-value pieces.
- **Practice with the exact calculator you'll bring.** Muscle memory matters under time pressure.

## 🧭 Free practice / community

- **SOA sample questions** (above) — the gold standard.
- **open-exam-prep.com** and similar sites — free FAM practice questions.
- **r/actuary** (Reddit) and the **Actuarial Outpost** archives — study advice, exam-day reports, and "how I passed FAM" threads.
