# Exam FAM — Detailed Overview

## What FAM is and where it sits

**FAM = Fundamentals of Actuarial Mathematics.** It is an SOA preliminary exam on the ASA (Associate) track. The usual order is:

> **Exam P** (Probability) → **Exam FM** (Financial Mathematics) → **Exam FAM** → then ASTAM or ALTAM, plus SRM, PA, and the VEEs/FAP modules.

FAM merges two classic subjects into one exam:
- **FAM-S (Short-Term Actuarial Mathematics):** loss models and general-insurance pricing/reserving.
- **FAM-L (Long-Term Actuarial Mathematics):** life contingencies.

You sit **one combined FAM exam**. (The deeper versions, ASTAM and ALTAM, come later and build directly on FAM-S and FAM-L respectively — so strong FAM foundations pay off twice.)

## Format & logistics

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Questions | **34** multiple-choice |
| Time | **3.5 hours** (~6.2 min/question) |
| Delivery | Computer-based testing (CBT) at Prometric centers |
| Sittings | ~3 per year (March, July, November) |
| Scoring | Reported **0–10**; **6 or higher passes** |
| Wrong-answer penalty | **None** — always answer every question |
| Calculator | TI-30XS / TI-30X MultiView (and other approved BA II Plus, etc.) |
| Provided | **FAM exam tables** — distribution tables + the **Standard Ultimate Life Table (SULT)** |

> Pass marks are set per sitting, so there is no fixed "X%". Historically, getting roughly **65–70%+ of questions right** puts you comfortably in passing territory — but treat that as a target, not a guarantee.

## Syllabus blueprint (approximate weightings)

These are the SOA topic weight ranges. They shift slightly each sitting, but the **relative emphasis is stable** — use it to allocate study time.

### Short-Term (FAM-S) — roughly 45–50% of the exam
| Topic | Approx. weight | Folder note |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Insurance & reinsurance coverages | ~1–10% | `01-Short-Term/01` |
| Severity, frequency & aggregate models | **~13–24%** ⭐ | `01-Short-Term/02–04` |
| Coverage modifications | (within above) | `01-Short-Term/05` |
| Parametric & non-parametric estimation | ~5–10% | `01-Short-Term/06` |
| Introduction to credibility | ~3–5% | `01-Short-Term/07` |
| Pricing & reserving (ratemaking, loss reserves) | **~10–15%** ⭐ | `01-Short-Term/08–09` |
| Option pricing fundamentals | ~3–8% | `01-Short-Term/10` |

### Long-Term (FAM-L) — roughly 50–55% of the exam
| Topic | Approx. weight | Folder note |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Long-term coverages & mortality/survival models | **~10–15%** ⭐ | `02-Long-Term/01–02` |
| Present-value random variables (insurance & annuities) | **~13–20%** ⭐ | `02-Long-Term/03–04` |
| Premium & policy-value (reserve) calculation | **~15–23%** ⭐⭐ | `02-Long-Term/05–06` |
| Multiple-state & multiple-decrement models | ~3–5% | `02-Long-Term/07` |

**Takeaway:** The biggest point-getters are (1) **aggregate/severity loss models** on the short-term side and (2) **premiums + policy values** on the long-term side. Get those rock-solid first.

## Prerequisites & assumed knowledge

You're expected to be comfortable with:
- **Probability** (Exam P level): random variables, expectation/variance, common distributions, conditional probability, moment/probability generating functions.
- **Financial math** (Exam FM level): present/accumulated value, annuities-certain, interest rates ($i$, $d$, $v=1/(1+i)$, $\delta$).
- **Calculus & basic statistics:** integration, differentiation, MLE.

If any of those feel rusty, brush them up alongside Week 1 — they recur constantly.
